• About

    The Rivendell Councilroom is a deck builder for the Lord of the Rings LCG by Fantasy Flight Games. It was built from scratch using AngularJS. Please refer to the project page for more information on the development of the Rivendell Councilroom.

  • How to use

    The Rivendell Councilroom is designed to be a simple and intuitive, yet fully featured deck builder. The left portion of the screen shows the cards currently in your deck, the center portion shows a card preview, the right portion shows the cards and some settings.
    There is no login and no information is saved on the server. Please make your own backups of decks, as nothing can be recovered in case of loss of information.

  • Cards

    In the Cards tab, you can browse cards to add to your deck. This tab also gives you the ability to search for cards by name (be sure not to use accents, type 'Gloin' to find Glóin, for instance), card trait, card text, card type and card sphere.
    You can also sort the cards by name, sphere, cost, willpower, attack, defense or hitpoints, by clicking on the relevant headers.

  • Packs

    In the Packs tab, you can select which packs you own. Only cards from these packs will show up in the Cards tab. The packs you've selected are stored in your browser data and will be remembered for subsequent visits to the deckbuilder.

  • Decks

    In the Decks tab, you can save the deck you've made (or are still working on) and load decks you've made in the past. Beware that clearing, loading or uploading decks will overwrite your current deck without prompt, so save your deck before loading another deck, or it will be lost.
    Saved decks can be downloaded, either in a text file that contains markup for both Reddit (MarkDown) and BoardGameGeek (BBCode) or as an OCTGN deck. Both these formats can also be uploaded. Note that uploading Does not automatically save the deck, it only loads it as the current deck.
    Decks are saved to and loaded from your local browser storage, so if you open the deck builder on another machine (or another web browser), your decks will not be present. Instead, you can either download the deck on the machine you saved it on and upload it elsewhere, or copy the link that appears in your browser's location bar as you save or load a deck. These links can also be used to share decks.

  • Stats

    In the Stats tab, you can inspect the deck you've made. The left portion of the tab shows you sample starting hands, press 'Start' to draw six cards and 'Draw' to draw additional cards. This should give you an impression of what a first turn would look like and what kind of opening strategies suit a deck. Be sure to press 'Start' after every change to the deck, as it is not updated automatically.
    On the right portion of the tab, several graphics are shown after you press the 'Reset' button. The top graph shows the costs cards in the deck, the middle graph shows the division of spheres in the deck and the bottom graphs shows the division of card types in the deck. Clicking on either a sphere or a card type will update to other two graphs, to reflect only the cards from that particular sphere or type. Again, after any change to the deck, or after loading a new deck, press 'Reset' to update the graphs.
